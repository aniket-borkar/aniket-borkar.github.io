Art in the Complex Plane

This visualization maps 50 works of art in the complex plane, where each work is represented by a complex number z = a + bi, revealing patterns in artistic expression across all four quadrants of the complex plane.

Context Normality (a)

The real part a reflects how conventional or unconventional the context of the work is. Values range from highly negative (unconventional contexts) to highly positive (conventional contexts).

  • a > 0: Conventional context (established forms)
  • a = 0: Neutral context
  • a < 0: Unconventional context (breaking traditions)

Cuil Level (b)

The imaginary part b represents the "cuil level" — the direction and degree of abstraction. Positive values indicate elevating abstraction, while negative values represent subversive abstraction.

  • b > 0: Positive abstraction (transcendent, elevating)
  • b = 0: Neutral abstraction
  • b < 0: Negative abstraction (subversive, unsettling)

Understanding the Quadrants

The complex plane is divided into four quadrants, each representing a different combination of context and abstraction:

  • Quadrant I (+a, +b): Conventional context with positive abstraction
  • Quadrant II (-a, +b): Unconventional context with positive abstraction
  • Quadrant III (-a, -b): Unconventional context with negative abstraction
  • Quadrant IV (+a, -b): Conventional context with negative abstraction

Fractional Values

Both the context normality (a) and cuil level (b) are expressed with precise fractional values, allowing for nuanced positioning of each work in the complex plane. This creates a continuous spectrum rather than discrete categories.

The magnitude of each value (distance from zero) indicates the intensity of the conventional/unconventional nature and the degree of abstraction, respectively.
