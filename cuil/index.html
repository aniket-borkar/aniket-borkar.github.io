Understanding Cuil Levels in Art

This visualization maps works of art in the complex plane, where each work is represented by a complex number z = a + bi, revealing hidden patterns in artistic expression.

Context Normality (a)

The real part a reflects how conventional or unconventional the context of the work is. Values range from highly negative (unconventional contexts) to highly positive (conventional contexts).

  • a = 2: Very conventional context
  • a = 0: Neutral context
  • a = -2: Very unconventional context

Cuil Level (b)

The imaginary part b represents the "cuil level" — the degree of abstraction, surrealism, or departure from reality in the work's presentation.

  • b = 1: Minimal abstraction, highly realistic
  • b = 2: Moderate abstraction
  • b = 4: Extreme abstraction or surrealism
